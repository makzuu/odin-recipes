French Toast

Description

French Toast, also known as "eggy bread" or "pain perdu" in French, is a classic breakfast dish made from slices of bread soaked in a mixture of beaten eggs, milk, and often flavored with vanilla, cinnamon, and a touch of sugar. The soaked bread slices are then cooked on a griddle or in a frying pan until they are golden brown and slightly crispy on the outside, with a soft and custardy interior. It's a simple yet delicious dish that is often served with a variety of toppings such as maple syrup, powdered sugar, fresh fruits, or whipped cream. French Toast is a popular breakfast choice that combines the comforting flavors of eggs, bread, and warm spices.

Ingredients

  • 2/3 cup milk
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract (Optional)
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon (Optional)
  • salt to taste
  • 6 thick slices bread
  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter, or more as needed

Steps

  1. Gather all ingredients.
  2. Whisk milk, eggs, vanilla, cinnamon, and salt together in a shallow bowl.
  3. Lightly butter a griddle or skillet and heat over medium-high heat.
  4. Dunk bread in the egg mixture, soaking both sides.
  5. Transfer to the hot skillet and cook until golden, 3 to 4 minutes per side.
  6. Serve hot.
